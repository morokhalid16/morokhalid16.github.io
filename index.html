The Atlantic Multidecadal Oscillation (P8)

Year: 2020

IMT Atlantique, Brest, France

Information

The Atlantic Multidecadal Oscillation is a low-frequency climate cycle, associated with the modulation of the sea surface temperature over the North Atlantic. The cold/warm phases may last for 20-40 years. It has wide impacts on the climate of the Northern Hemisphere.
I used model data from the CMIP6 archive to calculate the Atlantic Multidecadal Oscillation index in future climate projections.
I analyzed the differences in the index calculated using several simulations from the same model, and the spatial patterns of variability.
For more details, here's the github repo related to the project.
